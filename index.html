George Washington was an American soldier, statesman, and Founding Father who served as the first president of the United States from 1789 to 1797. Washington is revered as the president who set the standard for those who followed him, by serving the nation with dignity and intelligence.

Thomas Jefferson was an American statesman, diplomat, lawyer, and Founding Father who served as the third president of the United States from 1801 to 1809. Jefferson is best known for his role as the principal author of the Declaration of Independence in 1776 and for his efforts to expand the nation's territory through the Louisiana Purchase.

Theodore Roosevelt was an American statesman, conservationist, soldier, and writer who served as the 26th president of the United States from 1901 to 1909. Roosevelt is known for his progressive policies, trust-busting efforts, and dedication to conservation, as well as his role in building the Panama Canal and shaping the modern presidency.

George W. Bush is an American politician and businessman who served as the 43rd president of the United States from 2001 to 2009. Bush's presidency was marked by the September 11 attacks in 2001, the subsequent War on Terror, and the financial crisis of 2008. His leadership during these crises defined much of his legacy.
